# RBC Borealis — Let's SOLVE It Program Overview

## What Is It?

Let's SOLVE It is RBC Borealis's **free two-month remote mentorship program** for undergraduate students at Canadian universities. Teams of 3–5 students collaborate with RBC Borealis ML researchers and engineers to apply machine learning to real community problems.

The program is part of CIFAR's NextGen AI Training Programs, supported by RBC's philanthropic partnership with CIFAR to advance AI's societal impact and increase diverse scientific representation.

## Eligibility

- **Team size:** 3–5 undergraduate students
- **Enrollment:** All members must be currently enrolled at a Canadian university or college
- **Programming:** At least one team member must have completed an introductory programming course
- **Cross-university teams allowed:** Members can attend different universities
- **No ML experience required:** Genuine interest in ML is sufficient
- **Not limited to CS majors:** Applicants from all programs and backgrounds are welcome
- **Max applications:** A student can apply to a maximum of 2 teams

## Time Commitment

- **10 hours/week minimum** total commitment
- **1-hour team meeting** weekly
- **30-minute mentor meeting** weekly
- **Minimum 2 consecutive hours** available Monday–Friday, 9:00am–5:00pm EST

## Format

**Hybrid:**
- Weekly meetings are primarily **virtual**
- Students in Toronto, Vancouver, Waterloo, or Montreal must attend **at least one in-person office meeting**
- **Presentation Day** is in-person (mandatory if within 3-hour commute of an RBC Borealis office)

## What You Get

- Direct mentorship from RBC Borealis ML engineers and researchers
- Industry exposure and networking with ML experts
- Technical guidance and curated learning resources
- Career development support
- Free (no cost to participants)

## Expected Deliverables

| Deliverable | Required? |
|---|---|
| 15-minute presentation to RBC Borealis team | Yes |
| One-page summary report | Yes |
| Public code repository | Yes |
| Blog post | Optional |
| White paper | Optional |

## Team Structure

- One member is designated as **Team Captain** (primary contact)
- Captain's resume is required; other members' resumes are optional
- Captain handles all communication with RBC Borealis
